Earth Uncharted · Series Thesis

The world's greatest explorers didn't just embark. They built what it took to get there.

Earth Uncharted: Oceans Company is a social-first franchise built for premium TV. Each vessel takes us on a new adventure and a new expedition — from shipyard to open ocean. From the dark side of the moon to the bottom of the sea, we follow the expedition thought-leaders and pioneers of human exploration who refuse to accept that any part of this planet is beyond reach.

Bill Springer — Forbes Senior Contributor, Editor in Chief of Ocean Home, and a journalist who has sailed thousands of offshore miles — is the journalist-guide who reveals how that world works, and why it matters far beyond the wealth that builds it. He posts first at @billyswizzle. The audience is built before the network conversation begins.

"Season 1 starts at the shipyard. The franchise ends at the horizon."

Earth Uncharted · Season 1 World · Why OceanCo · Why Now

OCEANCO

This isn't a transaction. It's ignition.
Why OceanCo

OceanCo doesn't build yachts. It builds the future of what it means to go somewhere. The only shipyard in the world that has consistently pushed the boundaries of what a vessel can do — from Black Pearl's fossil-fuel-free Atlantic crossing to Leviathan's crew-first design philosophy and onboard scientific research capability.

Every vessel in their fleet is a working argument about what the ocean can become. That is exactly what Earth Uncharted is.

Why Now — Gabe Newell's Vision

In August 2025, Gabe Newell — founder of Valve Corporation and the visionary behind Inkfish, a marine science research organisation with full-ocean-depth submersible capability — acquired OceanCo. Not as a financier. As a builder.

"It's about plugging into a tribe of builders, dreamers, sailors, creatives and engineers and seeing what kind of future they can shape when no one's holding them back."

— Gabe Newell, OceanCo Owner, August 2025
Leviathan + Inkfish

The most important yacht OceanCo has ever built isn't just a yacht.

Leviathan — 111m, delivered November 2025 — is the flagship of Inkfish, Gabe Newell's marine science fleet. Onboard: a fully equipped dive center, laboratory, onboard hospital, 3D printing workshop, and facilities for scientific research in remote environments. A glass panel in the main staircase bears the names of nearly 3,000 contributors.

The Inkfish fleet includes R/V Dagon — a research vessel with a full-ocean-depth submersible that has already reached the deepest known parts of the ocean. Leviathan is the newest addition.

Why This Changes Everything

OceanCo is no longer just a shipyard. It is a platform for exploration.

Under Gabe Newell, OceanCo vessels are being designed not just for luxury — but for science, discovery, and the edges of what is physically possible on the ocean. That is the Earth Uncharted thesis in real life. The franchise and the shipyard share the same mission.

"Yachts have great potential to serve as platforms for scientific research. It's about recognizing that you're part of a broader community."

— Gabe Newell
